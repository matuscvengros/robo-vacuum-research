Technology

Spinning Pads vs Roller Mops

Spinning Pads
5 models: Dreame X50 & L50, Roborock Saros 10R, both Narwals
Dual rotating microfibre pads spin against the floor. Robot returns to dock for 75–80°C hot water wash, then resumes. Edge arms extend pads beyond the robot for wall coverage.
  • Low maintenance — rinse dock tray monthly
  • Pads lift or detach on carpet
  • Excellent edge cleaning via extending arms
  • Generally quieter during mopping
  • Pads get dirty between dock returns
  • Replacement pads every 3–6 months
Roller Mops
2 models: Ecovacs X8 Pro Omni, Dyson Spot+Scrub AI
A continuous wet roller cleans in real-time with internal scrapers flushing dirty water separately. Dock washes and dries roller. Dirty water never touches clean floor.
  • Superior single-pass hygiene
  • Dirty water never reuses on floor
  • Better on stuck-on grime
  • Dyson is bagless (zero bag costs)
  • Higher maintenance — weekly scraper cleaning
  • Can be louder (Dyson: 62–67 dB mopping)
Our pick: Spinning pads. With 80°C dock washing and multiple passes, spinning pads achieve equivalent sanitisation with far less manual upkeep — critical for a busy household with a toddler and dog.
